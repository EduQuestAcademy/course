Lesson 1.1: Asking and Answering Questions About a Story

Learn how to ask great questions -- and find the answers right inside a story!

Unit 1 -- Reading: Literature

What Are We Learning Today?

Have you ever read a story and wondered, "What just happened?" or "Why did that character do that?" That's your brain doing something amazing -- it's asking questions!


Great readers ask questions before they read, while they read, and after they read. And when you find the answer right inside the story, you are using a superpower called text evidence.


Today we are going to learn:

  • The 5 Question Words every reader needs to know
  • How to find answers in the text
  • Why asking questions makes you a better reader

Key Words to Know

Question -- Something you ask when you want to find out more information.
Answer -- The information that responds to a question.
Text -- The words in a story, book, or passage that you read.
Text Evidence -- A detail or clue from the story that proves your answer is correct.
Explicit -- Something stated directly in the text -- the answer is right there!
Inference -- A smart guess you make using clues from the text plus what you already know.

The 5 Question Words -- The "5 Ws"

Good readers use 5 special question words to help them understand stories. We call them the "5 Ws"!

Question Word What It Asks About Example Question
WHO A person or character Who is the main character?
WHAT A thing, event, or action What happened at the end?
WHERE A place or location Where did the story take place?
WHEN A time or moment When did the adventure begin?
WHY A reason or cause Why did the character run away?
Memory Tip: Remember the 5 Ws -- Who, What, Where, When, Why. You can also add HOW to make it the "5 Ws and 1 H"!

Read This Short Story

The Lost Kitten

One rainy afternoon, Maya was walking home from school when she heard a tiny sound coming from behind a bush. She stopped and looked -- there was a small, wet kitten with big golden eyes, shivering in the cold. Maya gently picked up the kitten and wrapped it in her scarf. "Don't worry," she whispered, "I'll take you somewhere safe." She brought the kitten to the animal shelter two streets away, where the workers promised to find it a loving home. Maya walked the rest of the way home with a warm feeling in her heart.

Let's Ask and Answer Questions!

Now let's use the 5 Ws to ask questions about the story and find the answers in the text!

Question Answer from the Text
WHO is the story about? The story is about Maya, a girl walking home from school.
WHAT did Maya find? Maya found a small, wet kitten behind a bush.
WHERE did Maya take the kitten? She took it to the animal shelter two streets away.
WHEN did this happen? It happened on a rainy afternoon after school.
WHY did Maya pick up the kitten? Because it was shivering in the cold and needed help.
Notice: Every answer came from the words in the story! That's TEXT EVIDENCE. We didn't make anything up -- we pointed to real words from the passage.

Where Do I Find the Answer?

Sometimes an answer is right there in the text. Other times you need to dig a little deeper.

Type What It Means Example
Right There! The answer is written directly in the story. WHERE did Maya go? The animal shelter -- it says so right in the story!
Think and Search Put clues from different parts of the story together. WHY did Maya feel warm at the end? Because she helped the kitten -- the whole story shows her kindness.
On Your Own Use text clues plus what you know to figure it out. HOW do we know Maya is kind? Her actions show it -- the text never says "Maya is kind" directly!
3 Tips for Great Readers:
  1. Ask questions AS you read -- don't wait until the end!
  2. Go back to the text -- reread the part that answers your question.
  3. Use the exact words from the story to prove your answer.

Try It Yourself!

Read the passage below, then try to write your own questions and answers!

The Big Game

Sam had practiced every day for three weeks. Today was the day of the basketball championship. His hands were shaking as he laced up his sneakers in the locker room. When the game started, Sam missed his first two shots. But he didn't give up. In the last minute, Sam caught the ball and threw it -- swish! The crowd went wild. Sam's team had won the championship!

  1. Write a WHO question and find the answer in the text.
  2. Write a WHAT question and find the answer in the text.
  3. Write a WHY question and explain how you found the answer.

Write your answers in your notebook -- then check them with a parent or teacher!

Remember This!

Great readers ask questions -- and use the story to find the answers!

Ask a question using WHO, WHAT, WHERE, WHEN, or WHY.

Look back at the text for your answer.

Use real words from the story as your evidence.

Sometimes you need to think hard -- that's okay!
