Lesson 10.1

Understanding Fractions: Equal Parts of a Whole

A fraction names equal parts of a whole -- learn to read, write, and draw them!

What Are We Learning Today?

Have you ever shared a pizza equally with friends? Each slice is a fraction of the whole pizza! A fraction describes equal parts -- how many parts you have out of how many equal parts the whole is divided into. Today we learn to read, write, and draw fractions.

Key Words to Know

Fraction -- A number that represents equal parts of a whole.
Numerator -- The TOP number of a fraction. Tells HOW MANY equal parts you have.
Denominator -- The BOTTOM number of a fraction. Tells how many TOTAL equal parts the whole is divided into.
Unit Fraction -- A fraction with 1 as the numerator. Example: 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/8.
Equal Parts -- Parts that are all the SAME SIZE. Fractions MUST use equal parts!

Reading and Writing a Fraction

Try It Yourself!

  1. A rectangle is divided into 6 equal parts. 4 parts are shaded. Write the fraction for the shaded part and the unshaded part.
  2. What is the numerator and denominator of 5/8? What does each number tell you?
  3. Draw a bar model for 3/5 and shade the correct number of parts.
  4. If a pizza is cut into 8 equal slices and you eat 3, what fraction of the pizza did you eat? What fraction is left?

Remember This!

Denominator (bottom) = total EQUAL parts in the whole.

Fractions on a Number Line

Place fractions on a number line to see exactly where they belong!

What Are We Learning Today?

Fractions are numbers -- and like all numbers, they have a specific place on the number line! Placing fractions on a number line helps us see their size and relationship to whole numbers. Today we learn to divide number lines into equal parts and locate fractions precisely.

Key Words to Know

Number Line -- A line with numbers placed at equal intervals showing their order and value.
Interval -- The space between two points on a number line. For fractions, each interval is one unit fraction.
Partition -- To divide a number line into equal sections.

How to Place Fractions on a Number Line

Step 1:

Draw a number line from 0 to 1. The denominator tells you how many EQUAL parts to divide it into.

Step 2:

Mark equal tick marks. For fourths, make 4 equal spaces (5 tick marks including 0 and 1).

Step 3:

Count from 0. The numerator tells you how many spaces to count.

Try It Yourself!

  1. Draw a number line from 0 to 1. Divide it into thirds and label 0, 1/3, 2/3, and 1. Place a dot at 2/3.
  2. Draw a number line from 0 to 1. Divide it into sixths and label all fractions.
  3. Is 5/8 closer to 0, to 1/2, or to 1? How do you know?
  4. What fraction is exactly halfway between 0 and 1? Between 0 and 1/2?

Remember This!

The numerator tells you how many spaces to count from 0.

0 is at the left end. 1 (the whole) is at the right end.

Equivalent Fractions

Different fractions can name the same amount -- discover equivalent fractions!

What Are We Learning Today?

Did you know that 1/2 and 2/4 and 4/8 all name the exact same amount? Even though they look different, they represent the same point on a number line and the same portion of a whole. These are called equivalent fractions!

Key Words to Know

Equivalent Fractions -- Fractions that have different numerators and denominators but represent the same value.
Simplest Form -- A fraction where the numerator and denominator share no common factor other than 1.

Lesson 10.4

Comparing Fractions

Which fraction is bigger? Use smart strategies to compare!

What Are We Learning Today?

Just like we compare whole numbers using >, <, and =, we can compare fractions! Today we learn three powerful strategies for comparing fractions: same denominator, same numerator, and comparing to 1/2 as a benchmark.

Key Words to Know

Benchmark Fraction -- A common fraction used as a reference point for comparing, such as 1/2.
Same Denominator -- Fractions with the same bottom number -- easy to compare! Just compare numerators.
Same Numerator -- Fractions with the same top number -- the one with the larger denominator is SMALLER.

Three Strategies for Comparing Fractions

Strategy 1: Same Denominator

Same-sized pieces -- more pieces = bigger fraction!

3/8 < 5/8

Both cut into 8ths. 3 pieces < 5 pieces.

Strategy 2: Same Numerator

Same number of pieces -- smaller pieces = smaller fraction!

1/4 > 1/8

Both 1 piece. Fourths are bigger than eighths.

Strategy 3: Compare to 1/2

Is the fraction more or less than half?

3/8 < 3/4

3/8 is less than 1/2. 3/4 is more than 1/2. So 3/8 < 3/4.

The Key Insight: Bigger Denominator = Smaller Pieces!

Remember: When comparing fractions with the same numerator (like 1/2, 1/4, 1/8), the more pieces the whole is cut into, the SMALLER each piece is! Think of sharing a pizza -- cut into 8 slices gives you a smaller piece than cut into 2 slices.

Try It Yourself!

  1. Use >, <, or =:   5/6 ___ 3/6   |   1/3 ___ 1/6   |   2/4 ___ 1/2
  2. Compare 3/8 and 3/4. Which strategy did you use? Draw bar models to check.
  3. Order from least to greatest: 7/8, 1/8, 4/8, 3/8
  4. Which is bigger: 2/3 or 2/6? Explain your thinking.

Remember This!

Same denominator: compare numerators -- more pieces = bigger.

Same numerator: compare denominators -- bigger denominator = smaller pieces = smaller fraction.

Benchmark 1/2: Is each fraction above or below half? Use this to compare!

Fractions of a Set

Fractions can describe part of a group of objects, not just a shape!

What Are We Learning Today?

So far we have used fractions for parts of a whole shape. But fractions also describe part of a set (a group of objects)! If 3 out of 8 students are wearing hats, then 3/8 of the students are wearing hats. Today we learn to find and use fractions of sets.

Key Words to Know

Fraction of a Set -- A fraction that describes how many objects in a group have a certain attribute.
Set -- A group or collection of objects.
Total in Set -- The total number of objects -- this becomes the denominator.

How to Find a Fraction of a Set

Rule: The total number of objects = denominator. The number with the attribute = numerator.

Example: 3 out of 8 balls are blue.

Key Insight: Finding 1/4 of a number is the same as dividing by 4! Finding 1/3 of a number is dividing by 3! The denominator tells you what to divide by for unit fractions.

Try It Yourself!

  1. There are 12 students. 5 are wearing glasses. What fraction is wearing glasses? What fraction is not?
  2. Find 1/4 of 20. Find 1/3 of 18. Find 1/2 of 14.
  3. Find 3/4 of 16. (Hint: Find 1/4 first, then multiply by 3.)
  4. A bag has 6 red marbles, 4 blue marbles, and 2 green marbles. What fraction of the marbles are red? Blue? Green?

Remember This!

Total objects = denominator  |  Objects with attribute = numerator.

Finding 1/denominator of a number = dividing by the denominator.

For 3/4 of a set: find 1/4 first, then multiply by 3.

Lesson 10.6

Whole Numbers as Fractions

Whole numbers are fractions too -- discover the connection!

What Are We Learning Today?

Fractions and whole numbers are part of the same number family! Every whole number can be written as a fraction, and some fractions equal whole numbers. Understanding this connection deepens your number sense and prepares you for more advanced fraction work later!

Key Words to Know

Whole Number -- The counting numbers and zero: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4...
Whole as a Fraction -- Any whole number written with 1 as the denominator. Example: 3 = 3/1.
Fraction Equal to 1 -- Any fraction where the numerator equals the denominator. Example: 4/4 = 1.
Fraction Greater Than 1 -- A fraction where the numerator is greater than the denominator. Example: 5/4 is more than 1 whole.
