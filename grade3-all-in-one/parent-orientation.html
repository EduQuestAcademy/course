Welcome, Parents!

A note before your child begins

Thank you for investing in your child's education with Owl Smart. This course was built specifically for 3rd graders at Sage Crest Elementary in the Kennewick School District — covering all the core subjects of a complete 3rd grade year in an engaging, self-paced format.

This guide will help you understand what the course covers, how to set up a healthy study routine, how to use the quizzes effectively, and how you can support your child without doing the work for them. Learning is most powerful when a student does the thinking — your role is to encourage, celebrate effort, and stay curious alongside them.

The goal of this course is not just to cover content — it is to build confidence, curiosity, and a love of learning that carries your child through school and life.

How Each Unit Is Structured

What your child will see in every lesson and unit

Every unit follows the same consistent structure so your child always knows what to expect:

Combined Lesson Page

All lessons in the unit are in one scrollable page with a sticky navigation bar at the top. Your child can click any lesson name to jump directly to it.

Inside Each Lesson

Every lesson has: an introduction, key vocabulary, clear explanations with colourful visuals, real-world examples, and a Try It Yourself activity.

Unit Quiz

After reading all lessons, your child takes a 15-question quiz: 9 multiple choice, 3 true/false, 2 fill-in-the-blank, and 1 open-ended writing question.

The "Try It Yourself" Activities

Each lesson ends with 4 questions or activities. These are NOT graded — they are meant for your child to work through in a notebook or on paper. Encourage your child to actually try them rather than skipping ahead. Writing answers by hand strengthens learning far more than just reading!

Recommended Study Schedule

Flexible options to fit your family's rhythm

This course is designed to be self-paced — there is no deadline. Here are three schedule options to consider:

Option A: Light Touch (1-2 sessions per week)

Session 1
Read 1-2 lessons from the current unit. Work through the Try It activities in a notebook.
Session 2
Finish remaining lessons, then take the unit quiz when all lessons are complete.

At this pace, expect to complete one unit every 2-3 weeks. Full course: roughly one school year.

Option B: Steady Progress (3-4 sessions per week)

Mon / Wed
Read lessons and complete Try It activities (20-30 minutes per session).
Fri / Sat
Continue lessons OR take the quiz if all lessons are finished.

At this pace, expect one unit every 1-2 weeks. Full course: 5-6 months.

Option C: Accelerated (5 sessions per week — summer mode)

Mon – Fri
One lesson per day (20-25 minutes). Take the quiz at the end of each unit.

At this pace, complete the full course in about 5-6 months of daily study.

Best practice: Short, consistent sessions (20-30 minutes) are far more effective than rare long sessions. Daily or near-daily habit builds real retention. Even just 20 minutes of focused study five days a week produces extraordinary results over a school year!

How to Use the Quizzes

Quizzes are a tool for learning, not just grading

Each unit ends with a 15-question quiz. Here is how to get the most from them:

Before the Quiz

  • Make sure all lessons in the unit are read — not just skimmed
  • Ask your child to explain 2-3 things they learned from the unit
  • Let your child take the quiz independently with no help

After the Quiz

  • Review missed questions together — the explanations in each answer are detailed and educational
  • Go back to the lesson for any topic that scored poorly
  • Celebrate effort, not just score!

About scores: The passing score is set at 70%. If your child scores below 70%, encourage them to re-read the relevant lessons before retaking the quiz. Getting a question wrong and then understanding why is one of the most powerful ways to learn — treat low scores as useful information, not failures!

Your Role as a Parent

How to support without taking over

DO these things:

  • Create a consistent, distraction-free study space
  • Sit nearby (you don't have to watch — just be present)
  • Ask your child to teach YOU what they learned — this is incredibly powerful for retention
  • Celebrate progress and effort genuinely
  • Connect lessons to real life ("We're driving past the Cascade Mountains — we just learned about those!")
  • Review wrong quiz answers together calmly

AVOID these things:

  • Answering quiz questions for your child
  • Rushing through lessons just to finish quickly
  • Making study time feel like a punishment
  • Comparing your child's pace to others
  • Skipping the "Try It Yourself" activities — they matter!
  • Letting your child skip units they find hard — those are the most important to work through

The "Teach It Back" Strategy

After your child finishes a lesson, ask them: "Can you teach me what you just learned as if I knew nothing about it?" Research shows that explaining something out loud is one of the most effective ways to lock it into long-term memory. Even if their explanation is incomplete, the act of trying to recall and explain deepens learning dramatically. This works for any age and any subject!

A Special Note: Unit 18 and Since Time Immemorial

Important context for parents

Washington state law requires that all public school students learn about the history, culture, and government of the Indigenous peoples of Washington — a curriculum called Since Time Immemorial (STI). Unit 18 of this course covers this material.

You may find that some of this content covers aspects of history that are difficult or unfamiliar — including the impact of colonisation on Indigenous peoples, the boarding school era, and the ongoing importance of treaty rights. This is intentional. The Washington State Legislature determined that all students deserve to know this history.

How to approach this unit with your child:

  • Approach it with curiosity and openness rather than defensiveness
  • Affirm that Indigenous peoples' cultures are living and thriving today — not just ancient history
  • If your child has questions you cannot answer, say "I don't know — let's find out together"
  • Consider visiting Sacajawea State Park (Pasco) or the Yakama Nation Museum (Toppenish) for a powerful real-world connection
  • Remind your child: learning difficult history helps us build a more just and understanding community

Technical Notes

Getting the most out of the course platform

Accessing Lessons

Each unit's lessons are in a single embedded page. The sticky navigation bar at the top lets your child jump between lessons without scrolling all the way up.

Best Devices

The course works on any device — tablet, laptop, desktop, or phone. For reading and writing activities, a larger screen (tablet or laptop) is recommended. A stylus or keyboard makes the open-ended quiz questions easier.

Notebooks

We strongly recommend a dedicated physical notebook for "Try It Yourself" activities. Writing by hand (rather than typing) significantly improves retention and is a valuable skill in itself.

Keep a Learning Journal: Encourage your child to keep a simple notebook as their "Owl Smart Learning Journal." They write answers to Try It activities, draw diagrams, jot down new vocabulary, and record things they find interesting. Looking back through it at the end of the course is a wonderful way to see how much they have grown!
