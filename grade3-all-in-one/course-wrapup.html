What Comes Next?

Ways to keep your learning momentum going

Keep Reading

Reading is the single most powerful thing you can do to build intelligence and vocabulary. Read anything you love — fiction, nonfiction, comics, magazines. Aim for 20 minutes every day.

Practice Math Facts

Multiplication fluency is the gateway to all future mathematics. If any facts still take time to recall, practice them for 5 minutes daily until they are instant. It pays off enormously!

Explore Your Curiosity

Did any topic in this course light you up? Space? Animals? History? Volcanoes? Follow that spark! Find books, videos, and museums about what excites you most.

A Final Note to Parents

Thank you for being part of this journey

You made a remarkable investment in your child's future by supporting them through this course. The fact that your child completed all 20 units says something important — about their character, and about yours.

A few thoughts as you celebrate this milestone:

  • Celebrate meaningfully. Acknowledge the effort your child put in — not just the fact that it is done. "I am so proud of how hard you worked" matters more than "you finished!"
  • Take a genuine break. Rest is part of learning. Let your child enjoy the sense of accomplishment before jumping into the next challenge.
  • Keep the habit. Even a small daily reading habit — 15-20 minutes — will maintain the learning momentum your child has built.
  • Stay connected to local history. Washington state is a rich learning environment. The Columbia River, the Cascade Mountains, the Tri-Cities history, and the living cultures of local tribes are all around you.
The research is clear: Children who receive engaged parental support in learning consistently outperform those who do not — not because parents give answers, but because they show that learning matters. You did that. That is a gift your child will carry for life.
