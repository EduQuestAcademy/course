Looking Forward

What Comes Next?

Ways to keep your learning momentum going strong!

Keep the Habit Going

The single most important thing you can do after finishing this course is keep reading every day. Even 20 minutes a day builds vocabulary, comprehension, and intelligence faster than almost anything else. Read anything you love -- fiction, nonfiction, comics, magazines, graphic novels.

Suggested Next Steps by Subject

SubjectGreat Next Steps
ELAStart a chapter book series you love. Keep a journal. Try writing your own short story, poem, or comic strip. Visit the Kennewick library and explore!
MathPractice multiplication facts daily until they are instant -- 5 minutes a day is enough. Try 4th grade math preview on Khan Academy. Use real money to practice budgeting from Unit 20.
ScienceGo outside and observe! Identify plants and animals, watch clouds and predict weather, find rocks and identify them. Visit the REACH Museum in Richland for local science and history.
Social StudiesVisit Sacajawea State Park in Pasco, the Yakama Nation Museum in Toppenish, or Hanford Reach National Monument. Look for local history everywhere in the Tri-Cities!

Did Any Topic Light You Up?

If you loved Science...

Check out books on animals, volcanoes, space, or weather. Try simple experiments at home. Watch nature documentaries. The natural world is endlessly fascinating!

If you loved History...

Explore books about Washington's tribes, the Oregon Trail, Columbia River dams, or WWII history. Your own community has extraordinary stories to discover!

If you loved Math...

Try logic puzzles, strategy board games, and number challenges. Maths is everywhere -- cooking, sports statistics, building projects -- look for it in everyday life!

Follow your spark! The subjects that excite you most are clues about your strengths and interests. Pay attention to what makes you want to keep reading and exploring -- that curiosity is one of the greatest gifts you have.
For Parents

A Final Note to Parents

Thank you for being part of this journey

You Made This Possible

Your child completing all 20 units of Owl Smart is a real achievement -- and it would not have happened without your support, encouragement, and investment. Whether you sat nearby during study sessions, celebrated quiz results, reviewed missed answers together, or simply made space and time for learning, your role mattered enormously.

As You Celebrate This Milestone

Celebrate the effort

Acknowledge how hard your child worked -- not just that it is done. "I am so proud of how hard you kept going" matters more than "you finished!"

Take a genuine break

Rest is part of learning. Let your child enjoy the sense of accomplishment before jumping into the next challenge. Celebrate fully!

Keep the daily reading habit

Even 15-20 minutes of daily reading will maintain the momentum your child has built. It is the single highest-return learning habit at this age.

Connecting Learning to Your Community

Washington state and the Tri-Cities area are rich learning environments. Now that your child has the background knowledge from this course, the world around them looks different:

  • Driving past the Columbia River connects to Units 14, 15, 17, and 18
  • Seeing the Cascade Mountains on the horizon connects to Units 15, 17, and 18
  • Buying apples or grapes at the market connects to Units 18 and 20
  • Voting season and election news connects to Unit 19
  • Meeting neighbours from different cultural backgrounds connects to Unit 20
  • A visit to Sacajawea State Park in Pasco connects directly to Unit 18
The research is clear: Children who receive engaged parental support in learning consistently outperform those who do not -- not because parents give answers, but because they show that learning matters. You did that. That is a gift your child will carry for life.
